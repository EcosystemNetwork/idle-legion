# Idle Legion — The Codex of the Deep

> *"They rugged the Surface. So we dug."*
> — first words carved into the Great Hall, author anon

This is the canon. Every room, gladiator, raid, and boss in the game pulls from
what's written here. Tone is **degen-comedy Roman apocalypse**: play the legion
mythos grand and bloody, then let the crypto-brain leak through. When in doubt,
a centurion would say *"ser."*

---

## 0. The One-Liner

A meme-messiah gladiator got rugged out of paradise. The world followed him
down. Now you dig a stronghold under a dead mountain, staff it with a legion of
gladiators, raid the wasteland for bags, and fund a **War Chest** that reaches
across every surviving Chain to hire mercenaries who only work for real money.

You are not trying to save the world. The world is cooked. You are trying to
**make it.** WAGMI. Allegedly.

---

## 1. The Setting — The Sundered Chains

Long ago (roughly one bull-market cycle) the world above ran on the **Great
Ledger**: a lattice of shining realms called the **Chains**, each its own
kingdom of light, liquidity, and unbearable posting. Value flowed between them
across the **Bridges** — vast humming arches of gold that let any coin walk from
one Chain to the next.

Then came **The Great Rug.**

Nobody agrees who pulled it. The priests blame a dev who "just went to get milk."
The soldiers blame a bridge. The bridge blames the multisig. What's known: on a
single cursed block, the Bridges were drained to the last sestertius, the Chains
cracked apart, and the Great Ledger forked into a thousand orphaned timelines.
The sky filled with the screaming of a billion stop-losses. This event is now
simply called **the Rugging**, and the age after it **the Sundering.**

The Surface — the sunlit world of the old Chains — became **the Wastes**: a
cracked overworld of collapsed marketplaces, feral validators, and roving bands
who'd sell their own mother for gas. Trust died with the Bridges. **Nobody
bridges anymore.** To cross a Chain on foot is to get exit-liquidity'd by
bandits before you reach the far arch.

So the survivors did the only rational thing. They stopped looking up.

They **dug.**

## 2. The Stronghold — Home of the Legion

Deep under a dead mountain, out of the sun and out of the mempool, the survivors
carved a vertical warren of chambers — the **Stronghold**. It is your seed, your
base, your cold storage. Everything you love is down here where the raiders and
the bots can't easily reach it.

The Stronghold grows **downward**, one chamber at a time, dug by hand into the
old strata of the earth (you can still see the fossilized candlesticks of ancient
markets in the rock). Each chamber does one job, and most are worked by members
of your **Legion.**

**The chambers (canon):**

| Room | In-world truth |
|---|---|
| **Master's Quarters** 👑 | The seat at the top of the deep. Your single greatest gladiator — the **Master** — holds court here while the legion below does the work. No slots, no upkeep: one throne, one champion, all the weight. In a legion that prays to Kekius, whoever sits this chair is *him.* |
| **Great Hall** 🏛️ | The heart. Where off-duty gladiators sleep, eat, and cope. Upgrade it to fit more bodies — a bigger Hall is a bigger legion. Above its hearth hangs a laurel and the words *GM.* |
| **Gold Mine** ⛏️ | Labor-hands chip **sestertii** (gold) out of the deep veins. The deeper you dig, the older the coin — some of it still faintly glows with pre-Rug value. |
| **Granary** 🌾 | Hunters stock **provisions** — grain, dried meat, and a little something the legion calls *hopium.* A fed legion mines hard. A starving legion posts through the pain and mines at half-speed. |
| **War Forge** 🔨 | War-hands beat scrap into arms. Produces raw **might** — the number that decides whether a raid comes home rich or comes home in a bag. |
| **War Room** 🗺️ | Where you plan raids on the Wastes. A dusty map, a lot of red string, and one guy insisting the top is in. |
| **Treasury Vault** 🏦 | The on-chain vault. See §6 — this is the one chamber that still touches the old Chains, and it's the only reason your legion has a future. |

**Incidents** are what happens when you dig too greedy, too fast (see §7). The
deep does not forgive apes.

## 3. The Legion — Ranks of the Deep

Your people are **gladiators**: fighters bought, freed, hatched from crates, or
born into the deep. Every gladiator has an **aptitude** — the thing they're
actually good at — and a **rank** that climbs from disposable to legendary.

### 3.1 Aptitudes (the SPECIAL of the deep)

- **⛏️ Labor** — born to dig. Best in the Mine, hauling sestertii.
- **🏹 Hunt** — born to forage and stalk. Best in the Granary, keeping bellies full.
- **⚔️ War** — born to break things. Best in the Forge, and worth their weight on a raid.

Put a gladiator on a job that matches their aptitude and they *lock in* (+25%).
Put them on the wrong job and they'll do it, resentfully, at base rate. Everyone
in the deep is a little overemployed.

### 3.2 Ranks (recruit → champion)

| Rank | Icon | Who they are |
|---|---|---|
| **Recruit** 🪖 | Fresh meat off the surface, still smells of the Wastes. Low output, high cope, cheap to replace. Everyone starts as fodder. |
| **Spearman** 🗡️ | Survived their first month underground. Holds a line. Holds a bag. |
| **Archer** 🏹 | Patient, precise, and absolutely will call you a paper-hand from range. |
| **Cavalry** 🐎 | Mounted, mobile, and expensive — the mid-caps of the legion. Surprisingly good in a mine (they haul with the horse). |
| **Champion** 👑 | Named heroes. Laurel-crowned diamond-handed monsters who've never sold. A single Champion can carry a raid. Kekius-tier. |

Gladiators gain **levels** by working and raiding, and get stronger with **gear**
(see §5). A Champion in full legendary kit is, in the technical parlance of the
deep, *him.*

## 4. Kekius Maximus — The Meme-Messiah

Every legion prays to the same laurel-crowned face. His name is **Kekius
Maximus**, and whether he was a real emperor, a real gladiator, or just a really
good post, nobody living can tell you.

> **The name, decoded.** *Kekius* is of **Kek** — the laughing god of the pre-Rug
> boards, whose green face the anons carved into every meme they ever posted.
> *Maximus* is Rome: laurel, sand, and a sword. Put them together and you get the
> whole joke and the whole canon in two words — **Kek in a laurel crown.**
>
> That is why every gladiator of the deep wears the same green face. They are
> **Keks**, sons of the laughing god, and their banner is the **Kek Empire**.
> This is the naming rule, and it is not optional: the people are Keks, the realm
> is the Kek Empire, and its emperor is Kekius Maximus.

**The legend, as the Great Hall tells it:**

Before the Rug, Kekius Maximus was champion of the greatest arena on the greatest
Chain — undefeated, jacked, laurel on his brow, a crowd of ten million anons
chanting his name across every timeline at once. He was the floor. He was the
vibe. He never sold.

Then the Ninth Chain got rugged out from under him mid-fight. The arena
collapsed. Kekius went down with it, sword still raised, screaming the last
honest words of the old world: **"WE'RE SO BACK."**

Three blocks later — the priests swear it — he **rose from the mempool.** Laurel
intact. Unliquidated. He walked out of the wreck of the Ninth Chain, told the
survivors to *dig,* and vanished into the deep to (depending on who you ask)
either meditate, accumulate, or touch grass for the first time in recorded
history.

The legion holds that Kekius will return **wen** the deep is deep enough and the
War Chest is full enough. Until then his face is on every emblem, his aegis is
the most coveted armor in the game, and *"stay Kekius"* is what soldiers say
instead of goodbye.

> **Two Kekii.** The deep also whispers of a dark timeline where Kekius never
> rose — where the Rug broke him and he came back **wrong.** That thing is not
> the messiah. That thing runs the Arena (see §8). Do not confuse the two. One
> is *him.* The other is the bear market wearing his face.

## 5. Gear & Crates — Loot from the Deep

Might isn't just muscle; it's kit. Gladiators equip a **weapon**, **armor**, and
a **mount**, each rolled from a rarity ladder the whole deep understands:

**Common → Uncommon → Rare → Epic → Legendary** (grey, green, blue, purple, gold —
the color language survived the Rug when nothing else did).

Gear comes from **lunchboxes** 🎁 — the legion's word for a sealed crate. Every
raid drops one. Cracking a lunchbox is the single most degen moment in the deep:
a circle of gladiators, one sealed box, and the eternal prayer *"pull gold, pull
gold, pull—"* Most of the time it's a Rusted Dagger. Occasionally it's the
**Kekius Maximus Aegis**, and the whole chamber loses its mind.

The named legendaries — *Emberforged Blades*, the *Kekius Maximus Aegis* — are
relics of the pre-Rug arena, worth more than most gladiators. They are, in the
old tongue, **grails.**

## 6. The War Chest & The Universal Account — The Last Magic

Here is the sacred part.

When the Bridges fell, cross-Chain value became impossible by any honest road.
Except the deep kept one artifact working: the **Treasury Vault**, and inside it,
the **Universal Account** — an oracle-relic from before the Rug that can reach
into **any** surviving Chain, gather whatever value lives there, and land it
where the legion needs it, **without ever touching a Bridge.** No arch. No
bandits. No "wrong network." One account, one balance, all Chains at once.

The lore reason this matters: **bridges killed the old world.** The Universal
Account is the one power that moves value across Chains *without* a bridge — which
is why the deep treats it as holy and the game treats it as the whole point.

- **Where it lands: Arbitrum.** Of all the Sundered Chains, only one stayed
  solvent, fast, and cheap enough to settle real trade after the Rug —
  **Arbitrum.** The Treasury Vault always lands its value there, as **USDT**, the
  one coin nobody argues about. In the fiction, Arbitrum is *"the last honest
  Chain,"* and settling there is how you prove a thing actually happened.

- **What it buys: the Free Company.** Fund the War Chest and you hire the **Free
  Company** — sellswords, mercenaries, ronin of the Wastes who do not accept
  cope, hopium, or promises. They take *real, cross-Chain money* and nothing
  else. In return they permanently boost **every chamber** of your Stronghold.
  More funding, better company:

  | Funded | Company | Boost |
  |---|---|---|
  | ≥ $0.10 | **Scout Mercs** | +15% output |
  | ≥ $0.50 | **the Company** | +35% output |
  | ≥ $1.00 | **Free Company** | +60% output |
  | ≥ $5.00 | **the War Host** | +125% output |

- **The Marketplace.** The same magic runs the on-chain **Bazaar**, where you buy
  grail gladiators and grails-tier gear with USDT settled cross-Chain, and sell
  your spare bags back for gold. Pay from any Chain, no bridge, no chain switch.
  The whole economy of the deep runs on the Universal Account.

**Player-facing translation:** email login → one click → funded → every room
climbs. The chain work is invisible; the payoff is a bigger legion. That's the
diegesis working.

## 7. Incidents — The Deep Bites Back

You can **rush** a chamber to fill it instantly, but greed has a price. Ape too
hard and the deep throws an **incident** that shuts the room down until your
gladiators fight it off:

- **Raiders broke in!** — paper-handed bandits from the Wastes, following the
  smell of fresh sestertii. They fold the moment the legion shows up armed, but
  they'll wreck a shift first.
- **Cave-in!** — the strata gave out. In the old tongue: *network congestion.*
  Everything halts, gas spikes (metaphorically), everybody waits.
- **MEV-rats infesting the deep!** — sandwich-bugs and vermin boiling up from the
  deep to skim whatever isn't nailed down. Exterminate on sight.

Incidents are the game's way of saying: *don't ape.* Or ape, and pay for it.
Degen's choice.

## 8. The Wastes & The Arena — Where Might Goes to Die (or Print)

Two ways the legion takes its might topside:

### 8.1 Raids — the Wastes 🗺️

Send an idle squad up the elevator into the overworld to take bags off the ruined
Chains. Bigger risk, bigger print. Each raid drops a lunchbox on the way home.

| Raid | The truth of it |
|---|---|
| **Outskirts Patrol** 🏕️ | Scavenge the village edge for loose coin. Tutorial-tier. Even recruits GMI here. |
| **Trade Road Ambush** 🛤️ | A light caravan carrying heavy bags down the old trade road. Hit it. |
| **Hill Fort Siege** 🏰 | Crack a warlord's walls and empty his cold storage. Needs real might. |
| **Rival Dynasty Raid** 🔥 | All-in on a whale's prize city. This is the send. Diamond hands only — this raid does not care about your feelings. |

The **Rival Dynasties** are the other survivors who dug *their* strongholds and
made it — the whales of the Sundered world. They are your rivals, your raid
targets, and the faces in the Arena.

### 8.2 The Arena — World Boss ⚔️

Under the ruins of the greatest pre-Rug Colosseum, the **Arena** still runs.
Warlords and worse are chained here, and the whole surviving world watches your
legion fight them for glory, gold, and rank. Beat one and the crowd of ten
million anons chants *your* name for a block or two.

**The bosses (canon), in order:**

1. **The Caged Beast** 🦁 — the warm-up. Something feral the old world locked up
   and forgot. It has never seen a candle chart and it is happier for it.
2. **Rival Dynasty Charioteer** 🐎 — champion of a rival whale-dynasty, here to
   remind you that other people also made it and they are *insufferable* about it.
3. **Kekius the Tyrant** 👑🔥 — the final face. The **dark-timeline Kekius**: the
   one the Rug broke, come back wrong, laurel blackened, wearing the messiah's
   face over the bear market's soul. To beat him is to prove your legion is truly,
   permanently **so back.** Him vs. *him.*

## 10. Voice Guide (for anyone writing more copy)

- **Grand first, degen second.** Sell the Roman apocalypse straight, *then* let
  one crypto word land the joke. "The last honest Chain" hits harder than "lol
  arbitrum."
- **Short in the UI.** Room and raid blurbs are read in a tight card. One
  sentence, one wink, get out.
- **Sacred words:** WAGMI, NGMI, GMI, ser, anon, ape, rug, mempool, bags, cope,
  hopium, floor, diamond hands, paper hands, *so back*, *stay Kekius.* Use them
  like seasoning, not like a whole meal.
- **The messiah is sincere.** Everything can be a joke *except* Kekius rising from
  the mempool. Play that one with a straight face. That's the emotional floor the
  comedy bounces off.

*gm. stay Kekius.* 🌅
